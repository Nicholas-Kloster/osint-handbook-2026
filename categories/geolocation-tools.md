# Geolocation Tools

> 27 tools — [osinthandbook.com/geolocation-tools](https://www.osinthandbook.com/geolocation-tools)

- [Bellingcat OpenStreetMap Search](https://osm-search.bellingcat.com/) — A tool for assisting researchers and journalists with geolocation tasks by querying public OpenStreetMap data.
- [Bellingcat Shadow Finder](https://colab.research.google.com/github/GalenReich/ShadowFinder/blob/main/ShadowFinderColab.ipynb) — A tool to estimate the points on the Earth's surface where a shadow of a particular length could occur, for geolocation purposes.
- [Black Dome](https://www.peakfinder.com/) — A platform with comprehensive information on mountains and mountain ranges.
- [DeepFind.me: Satellite Street View Search](https://www.deepfind.me/tools/geo-location/satelite-street-view-search) — Upload an image to analyze its content and attempt to determine the location. The AI uses the image's content for analysis, so results may not always be accurate.
- [EarthKit](https://earthkit.app) — A Nifty toolkit that helps you Geolocate faster with AI
- [FindThatSpot](https://www.findthatspot.io/) — A tool to search for relations between entities in OpenStreetMap.
- [Geograph.org](https://www.geograph.org) — A comprehensive collection of photographs capturing locations from all across the world.
- [GeoGuessr GPT](https://chatgpt.com/g/g-brlHi7t2R-geoguessr-gpt) — Geolocation expert for determining any location.
- [GeoHints](https://geohints.com/) — A collection of resources to aid geolocation.
- [GeoIntel](https://github.com/atiilla/GeoIntel) — GeoIntel uses Google's Gemini API to uncover the location where photos were taken through AI-powered geo-location analysis.
- [geolocatethis](https://github.com/musafir-py/geolocatethis) — An open source script that allows querying Google Maps API for two specific places in precise distance from each other within given radius.
- [Geolocation Estimation](https://labs.tib.eu/geoestimation/) — Estimate the geolocation of a picture by dragging or clicking the marker on the world map.
- [Geomastr](https://geomastr.com) — A web application designed to help users improve their skills in GeoGuessr that can also assist n general geolocation.
- [GeoNames](https://www.geonames.org/) — The GeoNames geographical database covers all countries and contains over eleven million placenames that are available for download free of charge.
- [GeoTips](https://geotips.net) — A collection of tips for GeoGuessr players.
- [GeoVLM](https://huggingface.co/spaces/AceXRoux/GeoVLM) — AI-based geolocation tool.
- [Manhole](https://manhole.co.il) — A database of over 12,000 different manhole covers from all around the world with photos, geo coordinates and notes.
- [Mapillary](https://www.mapillary.com) — Crowdsourced street-level imagery platform offering geotagged photos for mapping and analysis.
- [Netryx-Astra-V2-Geolocation-Tool](https://github.com/sparkyniner/Netryx-Astra-V2-Geolocation-Tool) — A street level image geolocation software.
- [Picarta](https://picarta.ai) — AI-based image location search tool.
- [Plant Net](https://identify.plantnet.org/) — A platform to Identify, explore and share your observations of wild plants.
- [Plonk It](https://www.plonkit.net/guide) — A searchable guide to GeoGuessr.
- [Plugger AI](https://www.plugger.ai/models/street-clip) — A robust foundation model for open-domain image geolocalization and other geographic and climate-related tasks.
- [ShadeMap](https://shademap.app/) — A visualisation that shows every mountain, building and tree shadow in the world simulated for any date and time.\n
- [ShadowFinder](https://github.com/bellingcat/ShadowFinder) — Find possible locations of shadows around the world.
- [Weather2Go](https://github.com/elliott-diy/Weather2Geo) — OSINT tool that geolocates screenshots using leaked weather widget data (time, temperature, condition).
- [WhenTaken](https://whentaken.com) — Practice geolocation by guessing when and where a photo was taken.
