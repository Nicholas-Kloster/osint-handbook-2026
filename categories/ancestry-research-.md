# Ancestry Research 

> 23 tools — [osinthandbook.com/ancestry-research-](https://www.osinthandbook.com/ancestry-research-)

- [Ancestor Hunt](http://ancestorhunt.com) — Offers a curated collection of free genealogy research guides, tools, and links to help users navigate historical records.
- [Ancestry](https://www.ancestry.com/) — Provides access to billions of historical records, DNA testing services, and family tree building tools for comprehensive ancestry research.
- [Archives](https://www.archives.com) — Simplifies family history research with an extensive database of over 11.8 billion records, including photos, newspapers, and vital records.
- [Cyndi’s List](https://www.cyndislist.com) — Serves as a categorized and cross-referenced index to over 318,000 genealogical resources, acting as a starting point for online research.
- [Family Search](https://www.familysearch.org) — Offers free access to a vast collection of genealogical records, including census data, birth, marriage, and death records.
- [Family Tree Now](https://www.familytreenow.com/) — Offers free access to genealogy records, public records, and family trees.
- [Find my past](https://www.findmypast.com) — Specializes in British and Irish family history records, including census data, parish records, and newspapers, to help trace ancestry. Also includes a free family tree builder.
- [Forebears](https://forebears.io/) — Offers a global database of surnames and forenames, providing information on name meanings, distributions, and genealogical resources.
- [FreeBMD](https://www.freebmd.org.uk) — A volunteer-run project transcribing the Civil Registration index of births, marriages, and deaths for England and Wales, offering free online access.
- [Genealogy](https://www.genealogy.com) — Provides tools and resources for building family trees, accessing historical records, and connecting with other genealogy enthusiasts.
- [Genealogy Bank](https://www.genealogybank.com/) — Offers a vast collection of digitized newspapers and obituaries.
- [Genealogy in Time](http://www.genealogyintime.com) — Publishes articles, research guides, and news related to genealogy, helping users stay informed about the latest resources and techniques.
- [Genealogy Links](https://www.genealogylinks.net/) — Aggregates over 50,000 genealogy links categorized by region and topic, serving as a comprehensive directory for family history research.
- [Genes Reunited](https://www.genesreunited.com) — Enables users to build family trees, search historical records, and connect with relatives through a platform focused on UK ancestry.
- [Legacy.com](https://www.legacy.com) — Hosts obituaries and memorials, allowing users to search for and share tributes to deceased individuals across the United States and beyond.
- [Locate Family](https://www.locatefamily.com) — Helps users find lost relatives and friends by providing access to a global database of contact information and public records.
- [MyHeritage](https://www.myheritage.com) — Provides access to historical records, DNA testing, and family tree building features.
- [Rootsweb](http://home.rootsweb.ancestry.com) — Offers a collection of genealogical resources, message boards, and mailing lists to assist users in tracing their family history.
- [Search for Ancestors](http://www.searchforancestors.com) — Provides tools for building family trees, accessing historical records, and connecting with other genealogy enthusiasts.
- [Sorted By Date](https://sortedbydate.com/) — Organizes obituaries and death notices by date, allowing users to browse recent and historical entries efficiently.
- [Sorted By Name](https://sortedbyname.com/) — Organizes obituaries and death notices by name, facilitating easy access to information about deceased individuals.
- [The Genealogist](https://www.thegenealogist.co.uk) — Provides access to a wide range of UK genealogical records, including census data, parish registers, and military records.
- [The National Archives UK](https://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/) — Houses the UK's national archive, offering access to historical documents, government records, and genealogical resources.
